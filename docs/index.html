We've used unstructured data to hack the learning curve.

At the heart of all analytics is the pursuit of finding meaningful patterns and connections in data. Structured data is information that has a fixed format and size, like numbers and dates. On the other hand, unstructured data is information that does not have rigid, organizing traits, like video, audio and images. Most data being produced today is unstructured. This means that research to help structure, analyze and uncover unstructured data is becoming more important than ever.

Our very own professor of analytics, W. Randolph Ford, has been conducting research on unstructured data, focused on finding the intent of the message. His studies on the matter have led him to focus on teaching systems that leverage our survival instincts to learn better and that use virtual reality and augmented reality to push education from short-term memory to long-term memory.
